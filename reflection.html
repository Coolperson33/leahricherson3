Rhetorical

I wanted to connect with people who might already have heard about corporate influence but didn’t really see how deep it goes. So, I started with a clear image of Trump with big CEOs like Musk, Bezos, and Zuckerberg. This helped me grab attention right away and show that big money really does affect our government. I kept the language simple so it felt real and relatable.

Material

I mixed up different types of content to make my point clear. I used that strong opening image, real numbers, and examples that people can see in everyday life; like how workers struggle with low wages or how media is controlled. I hoped these pieces would click with readers, letting them see the problem from different angles. Some parts worked better than others, but overall, I think using both facts and visuals really helped get my message across.

Methodological

My plan was pretty straightforward: show the problem first, explain how it affects us, and then suggest some ways to fix it. I built the essay in a step-by-step way so anyone could follow along, even if they didn’t know much about politics or economics. This helped me keep the argument clear and organized.

Technological

I had a few tools at my disposal: online databases for the latest numbers, digital libraries for research, and word processors with citation helpers. These made it easier for me to find up-to-date info and keep everything organized without too much hassle. Using these tools meant I could spend more time thinking about what to say rather than worrying about where to find the data.

Overall Impact

All these choices worked together to shape an argument that’s easy to understand and connects with people. I kept it simple, used clear examples, and organized my thoughts in a way that made sense from start to finish. In the end, this approach helped me build an argument that not only explained the issues clearly but also felt like a conversation with the reader.
