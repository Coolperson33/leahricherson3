The RP2040 and ATtiny412 microcontrollers have significant differences in architecture and features. The RP2040 is a dual-core 32-bit ARM Cortex-M0+ based microcontroller capable of running at up to 133 MHz with 264KB of SRAM and supports external flash up to 16MB for code storage​. It is optimized for performance and connectivity, with multiple GPIO pins, hardware support for SPI, I2C, and UART interfaces, and custom programmable IO for advanced tasks​. On the other hand, the ATtiny412 is a smaller, 8-bit AVR RISC architecture microcontroller with a maximum clock speed of 20 MHz, up to 256B of SRAM, 128B of EEPROM, and 4KB of flash memory​. It is designed for low-power applications and includes features like SleepWalking, which allows peripherals to operate in low-power modes, and has simpler peripherals like one USART, SPI, and TWI (I2C) interfaces​. While both are flexible microcontrollers, the RP2040 offers more computational power, memory, and peripheral support, making it better suited for complex tasks, whereas the ATtiny412 focuses on low-power, compact applications.
